Process

My workflow

01

Understand

Before touching any objective, I ask myself three questions: What problem am I hoping to solve? Who am I designing for? What are the user needs? From there I ask what I already know and what I am missing — filling those gaps in knowledge before committing to any direction. This prevents building on assumptions that will create more work later.

02

Explore

I always begin exploration by hand — sketching layouts, testing colour palettes, and trying different typographic approaches on paper before moving to any digital tool. This keeps the ideation phase fast and easy to fix. I look at reference sites both as inspiration and as examples of what to avoid, then narrow down to a direction that fits the problem and the audience.

03

Design & refine

Once a direction is set, I move into code to build a more polished and interactive version of the design. I treat the first coded version as a draft — something to test, critique, and iterate on rather than a final product. Refinement is where most of the real design decisions happen, as seeing something rendered at full scale reveals issues that sketches never will.
